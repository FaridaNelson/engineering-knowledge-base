Creating a Project Article

Whether you’re a recent graduate or an experienced professional, showcasing your work through project articles and blogs can be a game-changer.

Not only do they introduce you to a community and keep you active within it, but they also make you stand out to potential employers.

So, whether it’s a workflow that saved you 5 hours or a customer support bot that didn’t quite work the way you wanted it to, you should share your experience with others!

💡 Writing about your accomplishments at TripleTen is a powerful way to share your expertise with potential employers.

Communicating effectively in the tech industry
The ability to convey complex information to different audiences is a fundamental skill. However, making this information both understandable and engaging can be a challenge, even for experienced professionals.

Project articles provide you with a platform to hone this skill. By following the tips and structure provided in the section below, you will be able to create articles that can easily be transformed into verbal or visual communication when needed. This versatility can be a significant advantage in your career!

Embracing the "work in public" philosophy
The "work in public" philosophy has gained popularity among IT specialists for good reason. Instead of simply showcasing the final results of your projects or experiences, this approach emphasizes sharing your entire process, including the challenges and setbacks you encountered along the way.

By documenting your journey from problem formulation to solution, you invite feedback from other professionals and open the door to new ideas and approaches. This collaborative approach not only improves your work but also strengthens your connections within the tech community.

How to create your article
A strong article follows this structure:

Headline — the problem or question that the article is about
Intro paragraph — a summary of what you are going to discuss
Main body — all of the important information on the project
Conclusion — your results, with answers or solutions to the initial question or problem
Step 1: Headline
Choose the project you want to describe. To begin with, ask yourself this question: “Why is it important for readers to read your article?” Then, tell them in the headline!

Step 2: Intro paragraph
Provide a short summary of the story you're going to tell and describe the problem the project intended to solve. Try to link it to other people's concerns.

Step 3: Body
For the main part of your story, use subheadings to divide the article into sections. This will help you focus on a particular point in each section and make it easier for your readers to digest.

Step 4: Conclusion
Tell readers about your results! Answer the question that you asked in the headline or posed in the introductory paragraph. Include your thoughts on further iterations of your work.

Step 5: Final touches
You're almost done! Now, just check these DOs & DON'Ts before uploading your article.

Publishing your project article
Make sure that you review your article and check for grammatical or spelling errors. A simple way to do this is to read your article aloud. You can also record yourself reading your content and then play it back. Sometimes it’s easier to catch simple mistakes or awkward transitions while hearing the content instead of just looking at the words.

Before you publish your work, share and gather feedback from others in your network. Choose individuals who can offer supportive feedback on the technical content and your authentic tone of voice. By asking others for their feedback your are strengthening your writing and engaging your network at the same time.

Once you’ve checked your work and made the necessary changes, you are ready to post your article!
